Regional Parks in British Columbia

Capilano River Regional Park

Lush coastal rainforest and steep canyon walls make this an unforgettable​ regional park. As you walk beneath towering Douglas fir and western red cedar trees, listening to the roar of the Capilano River, it’s easy to forget that you’re in Canada’s third largest urban area.

Iona Beach Regional Park

Located where the Fraser River meets the sea, this regional park is known for its lon​g beach. It is home to a rare community of sand dune plants, and sits in the midst of the Pacific Flyway – making it a mecca for thousands of migrating birds.

Pacific Spirit Regional Park

The forests, creeks, beaches, cliffs and bog of this regional park provide habitat to a wide variety of plants and animals in the heart of Canada’s third largest u​rban area. The park’s trails are also a great place for Metro Vancouver residents to get out and connect with that amazing nature.​

 

Canadian National Parks in British Columbia

Yoho National Park

On the western slopes of the Great Divide, glaciers and waterfalls cling to the towering peaks of Yoho National Park. Discover the secrets of ancient marine life in a land shaped by water and human ingenuity.

 
Pacific Rim National Park Reserve

Lying along the west coast of Vancouver Island in the traditional territories of the Nuu-chah-nulth First Nations, Pacific Rim National Park Reserve boasts kilometres of long sandy beaches, unspoiled temperate rainforests and adventures that will delight the explorer in all.

Glacier National Park

With exceptional alpine scenery and deep valleys filled with ancient forests, Glacier National Park is a year-round paradise. Scale its heights following trails pioneered by legendary Swiss mountain guides, take a gentle stroll amid moss-draped old-growth cedars or hike through alpine meadows strewn with lichen-covered boulders.
